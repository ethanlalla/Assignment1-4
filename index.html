"A Summary on The War of Art"

I chose The War of Art because its all about understanding and fixing creative blocks, which I have been dealing with for years. The book really help me understand what causes resistance.

Steven Pressfield's interprits an artists mental block as something he calls "Resistance" . He writes about resistance almost personifying it at times. Resistance is anything that can stop you from taking on a task.

The book begins with a foreword written by Robert McKee . Written in the perspective of someone who previously read the novel, Robert simplifies and helps us understands what resistance is. By telling us true stories.

Resistance comes in many forms he states. Procrastination , is one such form. "I have an Olympic medal in Procrastination " Robert writes. And I counter that by saying I have 15 Medals in the sport myself.

"Resistance cannot be seen, touched, heard, or smelled. But it can be felt. We experience it as an energy field radiating from a work-in-potential. It’s a repelling force. It’s negative. Its aim is to shove us away, distract us, prevent us from doing our work."

The War of Art spells out exactly what every individual on the planet, creative or not, has to deal with , while also providing great advice on how to recogize and combat Resistance

Steven Pressfield breaks down this villain, whom holds us back from reaching our REAL potential.
